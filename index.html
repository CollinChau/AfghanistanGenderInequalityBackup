Fighting Gender Inequality in Afghanistan

Learn about the barriers Afghan women face in education, employment, and daily life and how you can help make a difference.

Symbol of Struggle

The Afghan flag has become a powerful image of resistance and identity for many women and activists fighting for gender equality under Taliban rule.

The Afghan flag has been used in protests to support women's rights.

What Is the Problem?

Since the Taliban regained control in 2021, women and girls in Afghanistan have been banned from attending secondary schools and universities. Most jobs are also off-limits to them. This extreme gender inequality affects millions and prevents the country from developing socially and economically.

Why It Matters

When women are excluded from education and the workforce, the entire country suffers. Afghanistan faces a low HDI, high birth rates, and a youthful population that lacks opportunity, especially for women.
