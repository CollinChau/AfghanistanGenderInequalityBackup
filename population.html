Population & Gender in Afghanistan

Understanding how gender inequality affects Afghanistan’s population structure and development. With over 60% of the population under 25 and a high birth rate, the country faces serious demographic pressure. Girls and women are left out of education and jobs, deepening the imbalance and slowing progress.

Afghanistan’s population pyramid shows a wide base, indicating a very young population. Gender inequality, especially in education and employment, prevents this youthful population from reaching its full potential.

Youthful Population

Afghanistan has one of the youngest populations in the world. Over 60% of its population is under 25. Without education or job opportunities for young women, this creates long-term gender and economic imbalances.

High Birth Rates

With limited education for girls and restricted access to healthcare, Afghanistan maintains a high fertility rate. This keeps the dependency ratio high, meaning more people depend on fewer working adults.

Gender Imbalance in Roles

Women are being systematically excluded from contributing to the economy, public life, and leadership. This isn’t just a cultural issue but intead it's also a demographic and development issue.

Connection to Human Geography (Unit 2)

This connects to topics like population pyramids, fertility, and the demographic transition model. Without gender equality, Afghanistan remains stuck in Stage 2 of the model: high growth, low development.
